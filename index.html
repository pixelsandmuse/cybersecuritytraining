What is Phishing?

info_outline
Definition
A cyberattack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information — passwords, financial data, or personal details — by impersonating a trusted source.
alternate_email
Commonly Appears As
📧 Emails from spoofed domains
💬 Text messages (Smishing)
🌐 Fake websites cloning real ones
Primary Goal: Steal passwords, financial data, or personal information — often to sell it, use it for fraud, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Password Security

straighten
Length Matters
Use at least 12 characters. Each additional character makes the password exponentially harder to crack.
shuffle
Complexity is Key
Combine uppercase + lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols like @, #, $.
person_off
Keep it Impersonal
Avoid birthdays, pet names, or favorite teams — hackers know to try these first.
fingerprint
Uniqueness
Use a distinct password for every account to prevent one breach from compromising everything.

Multi-Factor Authentication

security MFA adds a critical defensive barrier to your digital identity — even if your password is stolen, your account stays protected.
password
Step 1 · Something You Know
Password or PIN
Your primary credential — the first gate attackers must pass.
phonelink_setup
Step 2 · Something You Have
Authenticator App or SMS Code
A time-sensitive code sent to your device or generated by an app.
Enable MFA everywhere you can — email, banking, work apps, and social media. It's the single most effective way to prevent unauthorized access.

Key Takeaways

link
Verify Links Before Clicking
Hover over links to inspect the actual destination URL before you click — if it looks suspicious, go to the official site directly.
password
Use Strong, Unique Passwords
12+ characters, mixed types, never reused. Consider a password manager to keep track safely.
verified_user
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication
Turn on MFA for every account that supports it — it's the fastest way to dramatically reduce your risk.
notification_important
Stay Cautious of Suspicious Messages
Be alert for unexpected or urgent communications through any channel. When in doubt — don't click, don't reply, verify through official sources.
